Project Name: AlphaCas 8800

Project Functional Description:

We plan to create a miniaturized version of the Altair 8800, one of the first microcomputers. Our product is designed to appeal to hobbyists and educators interested in low-level computer programming, targeting these specific markets. Unlike other Altair 8800 clones and low-level programming interfaces, our product stands out by offering a compact, portable, and physical interface that authentically represents early computer technology. Furthermore, it supports ongoing learning through the potential for expansion cards and software development.

The replica will aim to maintain the functional structure of the original computer which includes an enclosure that has a front panel with switches and LEDs as a programming interface, and individual CPU and memory cards that will slot into a common backplane, which acts as an 8080-derived bus in the vein of the S-100. We will utilize the RP2350B microcontroller to emulate the functionality of the original Intel 8080 processor on the CPU card. If time permits, a UART card will be developed to enable our computer to do high speed serial interfacing. Our team will be designing each of the front, backplane, and card PCBs; the interfaces for the S-100 bus; a buck converter for power delivery; and software to emulate the Intel 8080.

Project Specific Design Requirements (PSDRs):

  1. PSDR #1 (Hardware): Design and implementation of mechanical constraints of various PCB connections. (i.e. Front Panel to Enclosure, Front Panel to Backplane, Backplane to Card)
  2. PSDR #2 (Hardware): Design and Implementation of front panel user interface, its connection to the card backplane, and clock/bus control protocol.
  3. PSDR #3 (Hardware): Design and implementation of a backplane integrated step-down power supply (buck conversion) from a reference design to supply 3.3/5V power.
  4. PSDR #4 (Hardware/Software): Design and implementation of S-100-inspired 8080 bus protocol via RP2350 PIO (Master and Slave)
  5. PSDR #5 (Software): Design and Implementation of 8080 emulator for use on a microcontroller with support for external clock.
  6. PSDR Stretch (Hardware/Software): Design and implementation of a UART card that allows for bi-directional communication to/from backplane bus to UART serial, allowing for the use of monitor programs.
